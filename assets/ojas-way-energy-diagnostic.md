## 1 · What the quiz is actually measuring

The five reports are not five points on one line. They sit on **two axes**:

**Pattern axis — the direction your energy distorts**
- **Wind** → *The Hyper-Drive Engine* — acceleration, wired-but-tired, dryness
- **Fire** → *The Friction-Point Profile* — heat, short fuse, inflammation
- **Earth** → *The Stagnant Circuit* — heaviness, inertia, flat motivation

**Reserve axis — how much Ojas/buffer is left underneath**
- **Empty (critical)** → *The Depleted Battery* — systemic collapse (the **override**)
- **Intact but thin** → *The Fragile Equilibrium* — functional, defended, paper-thin

The Depleted Battery overrides the pattern result because an empty tank must be addressed before its *direction* matters. The Fragile Equilibrium is the "looks fine, isn't resilient" profile — **not** an all-clear. There is no "you're already balanced" outcome; the intro copy must not imply one.

Scoring tags used below:
`HD` Hyper-Drive · `FP` Friction-Point · `SC` Stagnant Circuit · `DB` Depleted Battery · `FE` Fragile Equilibrium

## 2 · Scoring & routing logic

Each question has five options, one per archetype. Each chosen answer scores **1 point** to its tag. With 12 questions, each track can score 0–12. Total points always sum to 12.

**Routing (apply in order):**

1. **OVERRIDE — Depleted Battery.** If `DB ≥ 5` → **The Depleted Battery**. Stop. (Depletion across ≥5 of 12 domains is systemic and supersedes any pattern, even a stronger one.)

2. **Leading track.** Otherwise, take the highest-scoring track among all five:
   - Leader is `DB` (value 3–4) → **The Depleted Battery**
   - Leader is `FE` → **The Fragile Equilibrium**
   - Leader is `HD` / `FP` / `SC` → corresponding archetype

3. **Flat-profile catch.** If no single track reaches `4` (points are scattered, nothing dominant) → **The Fragile Equilibrium**. A managed, low-signal profile *is* the Fragile Equilibrium presentation.

**Tie-breaks (deterministic):**
- Any tie that includes `DB` → resolve to **DB** (surfacing the reserve problem is the safer, gentler call).
- Otherwise → the archetype the respondent chose on **Q12** (their own self-read) wins the tie.
- If Q12's tag isn't among the tied tracks → use **Q5** (acute stress response, the single most diagnostic item).
- Final fallback (rare) → priority order `DB > FE > FP > HD > SC`.

**The one knob to tune after live testing:** the override threshold in step 1 (currently `DB ≥ 5`). Lower it (e.g. `4`) to route more people to the gentlest, most nourishing report; raise it (e.g. `6`) to reserve the Depleted Battery for the most extreme cases.

## 3 · The test

> *Suggested framing line above Q1:* **Answer as you are — not as you intend to be.**

**Q1 — The first ten minutes of your morning:**
- Your mind boots up instantly — tasks, priorities, problems — before your feet touch the floor. `HD`
- You wake already braced and faintly warm, jaw set, ready to take on the day's friction. `FP`
- You wake heavy and reluctant, foggy and slow to clear, even after a full night. `SC`
- You wake already exhausted, reaching for caffeine just to reach a normal baseline. `DB`
- You wake fine — but only if the night went exactly right. One disruption and the whole day tilts. `FE`

**Q2 — When you finally have free, unscheduled time:**
- You "rest productively" — dense reading, planning, intense exercise. Stillness feels like a chore. `HD`
- Even leisure becomes performance — you track it, optimise it, turn it into a result. `FP`
- You sink into the couch, scrolling, snacking, sleeping — comfort that somehow adds to the weight. `SC`
- You're too depleted to enjoy it; rest doesn't restore you and joy feels far away. `DB`
- You can only relax inside familiar, controlled conditions — novelty drains more than it refreshes. `FE`

**Q3 — Your afternoon, roughly 1–4 PM:**
- You hit a cliff around 3 PM and push through it with caffeine, sugar, or willpower. `HD`
- Your patience thins; you turn sharp and critical, irritated by anything inefficient. `FP`
- A heavy fog rolls in; you feel sluggish and want nothing more than to lie down. `SC`
- Your brain collapses — simple decisions and basic emails feel like swimming through mud. `DB`
- You're steady, as long as you ate on schedule; a skipped or late meal quietly derails you. `FE`

**Q4 — Your sleep, honestly:**
- Light and easily disrupted; your mind won't hold still long enough to drop deep. `HD`
- You run hot at night, waking around 2–4 AM replaying conflicts and solving problems. `FP`
- Long and deep — eight or nine hours — yet you wake unrefreshed and heavy. `SC`
- Exhausted but wired; you wake at 1–3 AM unable to return, and sleep never truly restores you. `DB`
- Excellent under perfect conditions — but a noise, a temperature shift, any stress collapses it. `FE`

**Q5 — When something suddenly goes wrong:** *(most diagnostic single item — used in tie-breaks)*
- Your mind accelerates — more tabs open, faster, trying to out-think the problem. `HD`
- Heat rushes up your neck, your jaw clenches, irritation spikes immediately. `FP`
- You shut down, withdraw, and quietly put it off rather than face it. `SC`
- You have nothing left to meet it; you feel fragile, raw, easily shattered. `DB`
- A small thing triggers a disproportionate spike — heart leaps, breath shortens, instantly overwhelmed. `FE`

**Q6 — Your body's quiet signals:**
- Dryness — dry skin, restless digestion, cold hands, a mind that won't settle. `HD`
- Heat — acid digestion, skin flare-ups, tension in the jaw and shoulders, running warm. `FP`
- Heaviness — slow digestion, bloating, water retention, congestion, stiff joints. `SC`
- Erratic — sugar cravings, cold extremities, unpredictable digestion, no tolerance for stress. `DB`
- Contraction — tight hips, shallow chest breathing, cold limbs, digestion that reacts to every emotion. `FE`

**Q7 — Your emotional baseline lately:**
- Wired and quick, subtly irritated by slowness, delays, and anything that can't keep pace. `HD`
- Intense and exacting — a short fuse and a sharp eye for everything that's wrong. `FP`
- Flat and grey — not sad exactly, just absent of peak emotion or colour. `SC`
- Numb and apathetic; even empathy feels like a resource you've run out of. `DB`
- A low, constant hum of anxiety about something disrupting the order you've built. `FE`

**Q8 — How the people closest to you would describe you right now:**
- "Always on. Always producing. Never quite stops." `HD`
- "Brilliant, but intense — we sometimes walk on eggshells." `FP`
- "Quiet and withdrawn lately. Not quite themselves." `SC`
- "Running on empty, and still won't put anything down." `DB`
- "Has it all together. Everything perfectly in place." `FE`

**Q9 — Your relationship with routine:**
- What routine? You improvise at speed and let the day pull you along. `HD`
- Optimised and high-performance — every part engineered for output. `FP`
- Hard to hold; you keep slipping back into the comfortable and familiar. `SC`
- Mostly abandoned. Survival is the only structure you have energy for. `DB`
- Precise and non-negotiable — and breaking it is a genuine source of stress. `FE`

**Q10 — Your relationship with stimulants (coffee, sugar, pressure):**
- Fuel to keep the momentum going through a packed day. `HD`
- Coffee plus a competitive edge — it sharpens you, and it heats you up. `FP`
- You reach for sweet, heavy comfort food more than caffeine. `SC`
- Caffeine isn't pleasure — it's survival, and the crash leaves you worse than before. `DB`
- Measured and controlled; you're wary of anything that might tip your balance. `FE`

**Q11 — When an invitation lands that falls outside your plans:**
- You squeeze it in — your calendar is already overflowing anyway. `HD`
- You're selective; you've little patience for anything that wastes your time. `FP`
- You'd rather stay in; the couch wins more often than not. `SC`
- You decline — there's simply nothing left in you to give. `DB`
- You decline because it's outside your routine; you've quietly chosen control over connection. `FE`

**Q12 — The sentence that lands closest to the truth:** *(self-read — used in tie-breaks)*
- "I can't slow down, even when I want to." `HD`
- "I'm carrying too much heat and too short a fuse." `FP`
- "I know exactly what to do — I just can't get moving." `SC`
- "I've been running past empty for a very long time." `DB`
- "Everything looks fine — but I'm one bad day from it all falling apart." `FE`

## 4 · Worked examples (to validate the logic)

- **Answers: HD×7, DB×3, FP×2.** DB < 5, leader is HD → **Hyper-Drive Engine.** (A fast operator who isn't yet collapsing.)
- **Answers: HD×4, DB×6, FE×2.** DB ≥ 5 → **Depleted Battery** (override). The same fast operator, now genuinely empty — reserve beats pattern.
- **Answers: FE×6, FP×3, HD×3.** DB < 5, leader is FE → **Fragile Equilibrium.**
- **Answers: HD×3, FP×3, SC×2, DB×2, FE×2.** Nothing reaches 4 → flat-profile catch → **Fragile Equilibrium.**
- **Tie: FP×5, HD×5,** Q12 chosen = HD → **Hyper-Drive Engine** (self-read breaks the tie).

## 6 · Implementation notes

- **Email gate placement.** Highest completion usually comes from showing the email step *after* the final question and *before* the result reveal ("Your reading is ready — where shall we send it?"). The consent checkbox lives at that step.
- **Compliance.** No answer option or result screen should claim to treat, cure, prevent, or diagnose anything. Keep all language experiential ("you may feel…", "this pattern tends to…"), consistent with the reports themselves.
- **Trilingual.** Questions and consent translate cleanly; treat them as cultural adaptations, not literal translations — especially Q8 (how others describe you) and Q12 (the self-read sentence), which carry the most idiom.
- **Tuning.** After ~50–100 real responses, check the distribution. If too many land in Depleted Battery, raise the override to `6`; if almost none do, lower to `4`. The Fragile Equilibrium flat-catch can also be tightened (`no track ≥ 5`) if it's over-collecting.
